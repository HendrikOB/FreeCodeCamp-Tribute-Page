Prof Charles Robert Richet (25 August 1850 – 4 December 1935)

Here's a time line of Prof Charles Robert's life:

  • 1850 - He was born on 26 August in Paris
  • 1887 - Richet became professor of physiology at the Collège de France investigating a variety of subjects such as neurochemistry, digestion, thermoregulation in homeothermic animals, and breathing.
  • 1902 - He was involved in the French pacifist movement.
  • 1913 - His work with Paul Portier on anaphylaxis the term he coined for a sensitized individual's sometimes lethal reaction to a second, small-dose injection of an antigen won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.
  • 1914 - He became a member of the Académie des Sciences
  • 1919 - He expressed his racist and eugenist ideas in his 1919 book La Sélection Humaine.
